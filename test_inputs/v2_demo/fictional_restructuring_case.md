# Fictional Research Pack: Northstar Appliances Restructuring

## Situation Brief

Northstar Appliances Group announced on May 14, 2026 that it had missed a scheduled interest payment on its 2028 secured notes. The company said the missed payment triggered a 30-day grace period and that it was negotiating with Horizon Bank, Alder Capital, and a supplier committee.

Northstar reported that revenue fell 18% year over year after two large retail customers delayed orders. Management said liquidity was "sufficient for near-term operations" but declined to provide a full-year cash forecast.

## Stakeholder Notes

Horizon Bank is the largest secured creditor and holds liens on Northstar's main assembly plant. Horizon Bank expects any restructuring proposal to include stronger reporting covenants, monthly liquidity updates, and limits on new debt.

Alder Capital owns a large minority position in Northstar's unsecured notes. Alder Capital said a debt exchange could preserve value if suppliers keep shipping and customers receive delivery guarantees.

The Supplier Committee warned that payment delays could force smaller component vendors to reduce shipments within six weeks. The committee wants a funded vendor assurance program before supporting any restructuring plan.

The Employee Union represents about 2,400 plant workers. The Employee Union said layoffs would trigger public opposition and asked management to protect wages, pension contributions, and safety investments.

## Legal And Regulatory Context

The Commercial Court scheduled a status hearing for June 3, 2026 after a trade creditor filed a petition seeking appointment of an independent monitor. The court asked Northstar to provide a customer continuity plan and a list of critical suppliers.

The Market Conduct Commission requested additional disclosure about Northstar's liquidity statements and customer concentration. The regulator said investors need timely and balanced updates during the grace period.

## Market And Media Signals

Retail customers are considering backup suppliers because late deliveries would affect the autumn appliance launch season. One customer said it would keep orders with Northstar only if delivery guarantees are finalized by mid-June.

Industry media reported that Northstar's competitor, BrightHome Manufacturing, has approached several regional retailers with replacement capacity. BrightHome said it would not comment on competitor distress but confirmed it has available production slots.

Advertisers paused two campaigns featuring Northstar products after social media discussion connected the missed payment with possible warranty disruption. Northstar said warranties remain fully supported.

## Open Questions

Analysts expect three possible outcomes: a negotiated debt exchange during the grace period, a court-supervised restructuring if creditors disagree, or a liquidity bridge that buys time but leaves the balance sheet unresolved.
