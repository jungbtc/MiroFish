# OpenAI Deep Research: Northstar Restructuring Decision

## Executive evidence summary

Northstar Appliances reported that available liquidity covers only eleven weeks of baseline operations, while an immediate company-wide restructuring would require a larger up-front cash commitment. The filing also reported that management has not disclosed the board-approved downside limit for the program. [Northstar liquidity filing](https://evidence.example/northstar/liquidity-filing)

The secured lender group said it would support a reversible restructuring pilot if Northstar provides weekly liquidity reporting and protects critical-supplier payments. The lenders warned that support is not yet committed for an irreversible company-wide program. [Lender steering committee letter](https://evidence.example/northstar/lender-letter)

## Market and execution evidence

An industry benchmark reported that staged restructuring programs reduced cash burn by 18% while preserving supplier continuity during the first ninety days. The study cautioned that the result depends on a named operating owner and a funded vendor-assurance plan. [Restructuring benchmark](https://evidence.example/research/restructuring-benchmark)

A separate supplier survey reported that staged restructuring programs reduced cash burn by only 6% while preserving supplier continuity during the first ninety days. The survey attributed the weaker result to delayed internal approvals and unclear ownership. [Supplier continuity survey](https://evidence.example/research/supplier-survey)

The labor agreement permits a time-limited pilot but requires consultation before permanent site closures or material head-count reductions. [Northstar labor agreement](https://evidence.example/northstar/labor-agreement)

## What public evidence cannot answer

The external evidence does not reveal Northstar's board-approved budget, non-negotiable legal or security constraints, named execution owner, internal capacity, decision deadline, or downside tolerance. Those private facts determine whether Northstar should proceed now, stage a reversible pilot, or defer the restructuring.
